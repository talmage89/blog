In existentialism, authenticity is a term for your ownership over your identity. To live authentically is to live driven by an internal value system, eschewing social influences, authority, and victimhood. It's the idea of articulating the specific value system that is most true to your being and deriving your decisions from it.

Authenticity is very closely tied with living "true to yourself." This raises a few questions – who is the self that you are being true to? If the self is the set of actions that you perform, would being true to your self simply be a crystallization of the actions that you've already taken in your life? What dictates what direction your actions should move towards? If your current values are what drives the trueness that is sought, would aligning these values to fit your actions also be considered "authentic"? This concept is deeply rooted in the question of what the self exists as.

I've posted [an AI report on authenticity](https://blog.talmage.dev/posts/authenticity-ai/) that answers many of these beginner questions in detail, it's an excellent introduction to the landscape. The Wikipedia articles on [authenticity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Authenticity_%28philosophy%29) and [existentialism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Existentialism) are also a fantastic resource for broader familiarization with the subject. It's easy to talk about being "true to yourself," but understanding the foundation underlying these specific concepts and the knowledge they are built upon leads to a much richer conversation. It is important to research what has already been thought through.

One of the primary ways I'm moving towards authenticity in my life is following through on my desires. Following through on dropping social media, following through on improving my style, following through on cleaning out my closet. I hate when people constantly talk about wishing they could do something, or wishing they would finally try this one thing -- you have the ability to do it so readily available to you. Just do it. Identifying a desire to try something, remove something from your life, or be a certain way, but never making the time to actually do it is an inauthentic way of life. Make the time to follow through and to be true to your desires.

Another way I'm moving toward authentic life is self-expression. This is another form of holding oneself back, albeit through acute fear rather than laziness or friction.
